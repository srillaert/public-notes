# Pilgrims until we die  
Unending Pilgrimage in Shikoku  
by Ian Reader and John Shultz  
published 2021  
read 2025-10-14

The first study that I read about people who are addicted to pilgrimage.
Discovered this book through the interview with Ian Reader on "The Camino Podcast", episode 88 "Pilgrims Until We Die: Ian Reader, Shikoku, and Japanese Pilgrimage"

The backcover has a good synopsis : "
The Shikoku pilgrimage, a 1400 kilometer, 88 temple circuit around Japan's fourth largest island, takes around 40 days by foot, or one week by car. Historically, Buddhist ascetics walked it without ceasing, creating a tradition of unending pilgrimage that continues to this day. Some spend decades walking the pilgrimage, while others drive it repeatedly, completing hundreds of pilgrimage circuits. Most are retired and make the pilgrimage the centre of their post-work lives. ...
	... Examining how obsession, devotion, and a sense of addiction aided by modern developments and economic factors have create a culture of recurrent pilgrimage, this book challenges standard understandings of pilgrimage."

Keywords :
- Brocade fuda : fuda that signifies that one has done over one hundred circuits
- Kõbõ Daishi : the central figure of veneration in the pilgrimage
- Fuda : also known as Osamefuda, the pilgrim name slips
- Sendatsu : title of pilgrimage guide, one can apply after doing the pilgrimage four or more times
- Settai : almsgiving to pilgrims
- Shachũhaku : sleeping/overnighting in the car
- Shikoku henro : the Shikoku pilgrimage
- Shikokybyõ : lit. Shikoku illness, addiction to the pilgrimage
